1. Biological Science I
This subject typically covers fundamental concepts in biology, including cellular structure and function, basic biochemistry, and principles of genetics. It provides a foundational understanding of life processes at the molecular and cellular levels.
2. Biological Science II
Building upon the knowledge from Biological Science I, this topic often explores more advanced biological concepts. It may include the study of plant and animal physiology, reproduction, and development, as well as topics related to biodiversity and taxonomy.
3. Inorganic Chemistry
Inorganic chemistry introduces the basic principles of chemical elements and compounds. It may cover topics such as chemical reactions, the periodic table, atomic structure, and chemical bonding. A foundational understanding of chemistry is essential for comprehending biological processes.
4. Cell Biology
Cell biology delves into the structure and function of cells, which are the fundamental units of life. Topics may include cell organelles, cell division, and cellular processes such as metabolism and protein synthesis.
5. Ecology
Ecology explores the relationships between organisms and their environments. It covers concepts such as ecosystems, population dynamics, biodiversity conservation, and the impact of human activities on natural ecosystems.
6. Organic Chemistry
Organic chemistry focuses on the structure, properties, and reactions of carbon-containing compounds, which are vital to understanding biochemical processes and the molecules of life, such as proteins, carbohydrates, and nucleic acids.
7. Microbiology
Microbiology is the study of microorganisms, including bacteria, viruses, fungi, and protists. It covers topics like microbial structure, function, pathogenicity, and the role of microorganisms in various ecosystems and human health.
8. Genetics and Evolution
This area explores the principles of genetics, including Mendelian inheritance, DNA structure, and gene regulation. It also covers evolutionary biology, including mechanisms of evolution, natural selection, and speciation.
9. Biochemistry
Biochemistry delves into the chemical processes and molecules that underlie biological functions. It includes the study of enzymes, metabolism, cellular respiration, and the biochemical basis of various diseases.
10. Anatomy and Physiology
Anatomy and physiology focus on the structure and function of the human body. Topics may include the organization of body systems, organ function, homeostasis, and how the body responds to various physiological challenges.
