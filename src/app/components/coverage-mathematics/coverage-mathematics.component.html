LET Mathematics Coverage

1. Arithmetic and Number Theory and Business Math

This topic encompasses the fundamental principles of arithmetic, including operations with whole numbers, fractions, decimals, and percentages. Number theory explores properties of integers, prime numbers, and divisibility. Business math involves applying mathematical concepts to real-world financial and economic scenarios, including topics like interest, profit and loss, and financial management.

2. Basic and Advanced Algebra

Basic algebra introduces concepts such as linear equations, inequalities, and quadratic equations. Advanced algebra extends these principles to include polynomial functions, rational functions, and exponential and logarithmic equations, providing a foundation for more complex mathematical analysis and problem-solving.

3. Plane and Solid Geometry

Plane geometry focuses on the properties and relationships of shapes and figures in two dimensions, while solid geometry extends these concepts to three-dimensional objects. Topics include angles, triangles, polygons, circles, and volumes of geometric solids.

4. Trigonometry

Trigonometry deals with the study of angles and the relationships between angles and sides of triangles. It has applications in various fields, such as engineering, physics, and navigation, and includes topics like trigonometric functions, identities, and solving trigonometric equations.

5. Probability and Statistics

Probability explores the likelihood of events occurring, while statistics involves collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data. Topics include probability distributions, statistical measures, hypothesis testing, and data representation.

6. Analytical Geometry

Analytical geometry combines algebra and geometry to study geometric objects using coordinates. It includes equations of lines, conic sections, and transformations of geometric shapes.

7. Calculus

Calculus is the study of rates of change and accumulation. It includes differential calculus, which deals with derivatives and rates of change, and integral calculus, which focuses on finding areas and accumulations.

8. Modern Geometry, Linear and Abstract Algebra

Modern geometry explores non-Euclidean geometries and their applications. Linear algebra deals with vector spaces, matrices, and linear transformations, while abstract algebra delves into algebraic structures like groups, rings, and fields.

9. History of Mathematics

This topic examines the historical development of mathematical concepts and the contributions of mathematicians throughout history, providing context for the evolution of mathematical thought.

10. Problem Solving, Mathematical Investigation, Instrumentation, and Assessment

This area emphasizes problem-solving strategies, mathematical investigations, the use of mathematical tools and technology, and methods for assessing student understanding and performance in mathematics.
