Stages of Human Development and Erikson's Psychosocial Crisis Theory

  1. Infancy (birth to 18 months)
    Basic Conflict: Trust vs. Mistrust
    Important Events: Feeding
    Outcome: Hope
    During the first stage of psychosocial development, children develop a sense of trust when caregivers provide reliability, care, and affection. A lack of this will lead to mistrust.
  2. Stage: Early Childhood (2 to 3 years)
    Basic Conflict: Autonomy vs. Shame and Doubt
    Important Events: Toilet Training
    Outcome: Will
    Children need to develop a sense of personal control over physical skills and a sense of independence. Success leads to feelings of autonomy, failure results in feelings of shame and doubt.
  3. Stage: Preschool (3 to 5 years)
    Basic Conflict: Initiative vs. Guilt
    Important Events: Exploration
    Outcome: Purpose
    Children need to begin asserting control and power over the environment. Success in this stage leads to a sense of purpose. Children who try to exert too much power experience disapproval, resulting in a sense of guilt.
  4. Stage: School Age (6 to 11 years)
    Basic Conflict: Industry vs. Inferiority
    Important Events: School
    Outcome: Confidence
    Children need to cope with new social and academic demands. Success leads to a sense of competence, while failure results in feelings of inferiority.
  5. Stage: Adolescence (12 to 18 years)
    Basic Conflict: Identity vs. Role Confusion
    Important Events: Social Relationships
    Outcome: Fidelity
    Teens need to develop a sense of self and personal identity. Success leads to an ability to stay true to yourself, while failure leads to role confusion and a weak sense of self.
  6. Stage: Young Adulthood (19 to 40 years)
    Basic Conflict: Intimacy vs. Isolation
    Important Events: Relationships
    Outcome: Love
    Young adults need to form intimate, loving relationships with other people. Success leads to strong relationships, while failure results in loneliness and isolation.
  7. Stage: Middle Adulthood (40 to 65 years)
    Basic Conflict: Generativity vs. Stagnation
    Important Events: Work and Parenthood
    Outcome: Care
    Adults need to create or nurture things that will outlast them, often by having children or creating a positive change that benefits other people. Success leads to feelings of usefulness and accomplishment, while failure results in shallow involvement in the world.
  8. Stage: Maturity (65 to death)
    Basic Conflict: Ego Integrity vs. Despair
    Important Events: Reflection on life
    Outcome: Wisdom
    Older adults need to look back on life and feel a sense of fulfillment. Success at this stage leads to feelings of wisdom, while failure results in regret, bitterness, and despair.

Foundations of Holistic Learning: Exploring the Four Pillars of Education

  1. Learning to Know

    Focuses on combining broad general knowledge and basic education with the opportunity to work on a small number of subjects in light of rapid changes brought about by scientific progress and new forms of economic and social activity.

    It emphasizes learning how to learn and discover, as well as benefiting from ongoing educational opportunities continuously arising throughout life.

    The goals include developing the faculties of memory, imagination, reasoning, and problem-solving, as well as understanding one's environment and communicating effectively with others.

  2. Learning to Do

    Emphasizes the learning of skills necessary to practice a profession or trade, applying in practice what has been learned.

    It aims to develop vocational, occupational, and technical skills, along with social skills for building meaningful interpersonal relationships.

    The focus is on developing competence, social behavior, aptitude for teamwork, enhancing the ability to communicate and work with others, and managing and resolving conflicts.

  3. Learning to Be

    Prioritizes the development of human potential to the fullest, tapping into the hidden talents within individuals. It aims at developing personal commitment and responsibility for the common good.

  4. Learning to Live Together

    Emphasizes understanding others, their history, traditions, and cultures, fostering living and interacting peacefully together.

    It promotes appreciating the diversity of the human race, being receptive to others through dialogue and debate, caring about others, and working toward common objectives in cooperative undertakings.

    The focus also includes managing and resolving conflicts.

Understanding Gestalt Principles in Cognitive Perspective

Definition:The term "Gestalt" originates from the German word meaning "whole, form, pattern, or configuration."

Focus:This cognitive theory centers on perception and how individuals attribute meaning to visual stimuli, emphasizing that "the whole is more than the sum of all parts."

Laws of Perception:
  1. Law of Proximity - Elements close together are perceived as a coherent object.
  2. Law of Similarity - Similar elements are perceived as part of the same form.
  3. Law of Closure - Gaps in the figure are ignored.
  4. Law of Continuation - Patterns establish an implied direction, with people preferring a continuous line.
  5. Law of Pragnanz - Stimuli are organized into the simplest and most cohesive figure possible.
  6. Law of Figure/Ground - Attention is directed to and perception is focused on things in the foreground first.

Insight Learning:Gestalt theory aligns with the concept that learning occurs through discovery.

Psychological Effects in Action: Understanding Behavioral Phenomena

Ripple Effect - The spreading effect of a series of consequences caused by a single action or event.

Hawthorne Effect - A type of reactivity effect in which individuals improve an aspect of their behavior in response to their awareness of being observed.

Halo Effect - A cognitive bias in which an observer's overall impression of a person influences the observer's feelings and thoughts about the entity's character or property.

Pygmalion Effect - Demonstrates the teacher's expectations leading to self-fulfillment.

Golem Effect - Low expectations lead to a decrease in performance.

Key Republic Acts Shaping Philippine Education: A Concise Overview

  1. Republic Act 9155 - Governance of Basic Education Act of 2001

    Governs the basic education system in the Philippines.

  2. Republic Act 6728 - GASTPE (Government Assistance to Students and Teachers in Private Education) Act

    Provides government assistance to students and teachers in private education.

  3. Republic Act 7722 - Higher Education Act of 1994

    Creates the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) to oversee higher education institutions.

  4. Republic Act 7784 - CHED Center of Excellence Act

    Establishes centers of excellence within the Commission on Higher Education.

  5. Republic Act 7796 - Technical Education and Skills Development Act of 1994

    Establishes the Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA) to manage technical education and skills development.

  6. Republic Act 6655 - Free Public Secondary Education Act of 1988

    Ensures free public secondary education in the Philippines.

  7. Republic Act 4090 - State Scholarship Council Act

    Creates a council to integrate, systematize, administer, and implement scholarship programs.

  8. Republic Act 5447 - Special Education Fund Act of 1968

    Establishes a fund for special education programs.

  9. Republic Act 6139 - Regulation of Secretarian and Private Schools Act

    Regulates tuition fees of private educational institutions.

  10. Republic Act 7687 - Science and Technology Scholarship Act of 1994

    Provides scholarships for science and technology students.

  11. Republic Act 7743 - Establishment of City and Municipal Libraries Act

    Establishes libraries in cities and municipalities.

  12. Republic Act 8292 - Higher Education Modernization Act of 1997

    Modernizes higher education institutions.

  13. Republic Act 6850 - Granting Civil Service Eligibility Act

    Grants civil service eligibility to government employees under certain conditions.

  14. Republic Act 8545 - Amending RA 7628 Expanded GASTPE Act

    Amends the Expanded GASTPE Act for additional assistance.

  15. Republic Act 8525 - Adopt-a-School Program Act

    Establishes a program for private sector participation in public school improvement.

  16. Republic Act 8491 - Flag and Heraldic Code of the Philippines

    Governs the use of the Philippine flag and heraldic items.

  17. Republic Act 7797 - Lengthening the School Program Act

    Extends the school program to 200-220 days.

  18. Republic Act 8190 - Priority for Residents in School Appointments Act

    Prioritizes residents in the appointment and assignment of school personnel.

  19. Republic Act 6972 - Day Care Center for Every Barangay Act

    Establishes day care centers in every barangay.

  20. Republic Act 7624 - Drug Prevention and Control Integration in Curricula Act

    Integrates drug prevention and control in intermediate and secondary curricula.

  21. Republic Act 7877 - Anti-Sexual Harassment Act of 1995

    Addresses and prevents sexual harassment in educational institutions.

  22. Republic Act 9163 - National Service Training Program (NSTP) Act of 2001

    Mandates national service training for tertiary level students.

  23. Republic Act 6193 - Regulation of Tuition Fees of Private Education Institutions Act

    Regulates tuition fees of private educational institutions.

  24. Republic Act 10627 - Anti-Bullying Act of 2013

    Addresses and prevents bullying in schools.

  25. Republic Act 10533 - Enhanced Basic Education Act of 2013 (K-12 Program)

    Enhances the basic education system through the K-12 program.

  26. Republic Act 9485 - Anti-Red Tape Act

    Aims to reduce bureaucratic red tape in government transactions.

  27. Executive Order (E.O.) 66 - Rule of Cancellation of Classes Act

    Provides guidelines for the cancellation of classes due to calamities.
