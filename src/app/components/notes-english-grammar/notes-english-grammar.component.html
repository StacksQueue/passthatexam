English - Grammar

Nouns

A noun names something- person, place, things, events, phenomena, emotions, etc.

  1. Proper Nouns - Names of specific persons, things, or places.

    Examples: John, Eiffel Tower, Google

  2. Common Nouns - Refer to any one of a class of persons, places, or things.

    Examples: dog, city, book

  3. Count Nouns - Nouns that can be counted and quantified using numbers.

    Examples: three apples, five cars, ten students

  4. Non-Count Nouns (Mass Nouns) - Nouns that cannot be counted and quantified using measurements.

    Examples: water, happiness, information

  5. Collective Nouns - Nouns that name a group of persons or things.

    Examples: team, flock, family

  6. Compound Nouns - Nouns that are made up of two words acting as a single unit.

    Examples: toothpaste, swimming pool, bookshelf

Verbs

  1. Action Verbs - These are verbs that express action. A majority of verbs are of this nature, and that is why most of the time, learners tend to define verbs as action words. Words like run, walk, talk, sing, dance, etc. are common examples of action verbs. We can further classify actions verbs into two more sub classifications-Regular and Irregular verbs.
    1. Regular verbs - Action verb is considered regular if its past form is derived by adding d or ed. Let us take the word walk for an example; the past form of walk is walked.
      Base Form Past Form
      Watch Watched
      Bake Baked
      Pick Picked
      Save Saved
    2. Irregular verbs - Action verb is considered irregular if its past form is derived not by adding d or ed, but through a change or through retention in spelling.
      Base Form Past Form
      Pay Paid
      Put Put
      Say Said
      Write Wrote
