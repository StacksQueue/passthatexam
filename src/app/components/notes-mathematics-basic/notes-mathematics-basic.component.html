Integers

  1. Real numbers - all numbers having decimal representations.
    1. 5 (An integer is also a real number.)
    2. √2 (An irrational number, but still a real number.)
  2. Rational numbers - all numbers that are a quotient of two integers whose divisor is not equal to zero.
    1. 3⁄4 (Quotient of integers 3 and 4)
    2. -5⁄2 (Quotient of integers -5 and 2)
  3. Irrational numbers - any number that is not rational and represented by a non-repeating decimal.
    1. π (The mathematical constant pi)
    2. √3 (The square root of 3, which cannot be expressed as a fraction)

    1. {{ "(-3) + (-6) = -9" }}

    In adding integers with opposite signs, subtract the numbers and copy the sign of the larger number.

    1. {{ "(-12) + 8 = -4" }}

  2. Subtraction - In subtracting integers, change the sign of the subtrahend. Then, proceed to the steps in adding integers.
    1. {{ "14 – (-5) = 14 + 5 = 19" }}
    2. {{ "-8 – (-10) = -8 + (-10) = -18" }}

    1. {{ "(-3) (-3) = 9" }}

Law of Addition and Multiplication

  1. Commutative Law of Addition - The order of adding two numbers does not affect the result.
    1. A + B = B + A
  2. Associative Law of Addition - The grouping of three numbers in an addition operation does not affect the result.
    1. A + (B + C) = (A + B) + C
  3. Commutative Law of Multiplication - The order of multiplying two numbers does not affect the result.
    1. AB = BA
  4. Associative Law of Multiplication - The grouping of three numbers in a multiplication operation does not affect the result.
    1. A(BC) = (AB)C
  5. Distributive Law - Multiplying a sum by a number is the same as multiplying each term by the number and then adding the results.
    1. (A + B)C = AC + BC

Least Common Multiple (LCM)

The LCM of two or more numbers is the smallest number which is the common multiple among the numbers.

Find the LCM of 18 and 24

  1. Prime factorization:
    18 = 2 × 3 × 3
    24 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3
  2. TList the prime factors, including all the unique prime factors from both numbers:
    18=2 × 3 × 3 and 24=2 × 2 × 2 × 3
  3. The common factors are 2 and 3. Include them once, and also include the remaining factors from each number:
    LCM=2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 = 72

Find the LCM of 15 and 20

  1. Prime factorization:
    15 = 3 × 5
    24 = 20 =2 × 2 × 5
  2. List the prime factors, including all the unique prime factors from both numbers:
    15 = 3 × 5 and 20 = 2 × 2 × 5
  3. The common factor is 5. Include it once, and also include the remaining factors from each number:
    LCM=2 × 2 × 3 × 5 = 60

Divisibility

A number is divisible by a certain number if the remainder of their quotient is equal to zero.

  1. Divisibility by 2 - even number
    1. 22 is an even number, therefore it is divisible by 2
    2. 36 is an even number, therefore it is divisible by 2
  2. Divisibility by 3 - sum of digits is divisible by 3
    1. The sum of the number 111 is equal to three (1+1+1 = 3), which is divisible by 3, therefore 111 is divisible by 3.
    2. The sum of the number 312 is equal to six (3+1+2 = 6), which is divisible by 3, therefore 312 is divisible by 3.
  3. Divisibility by 4 - last two digits are divisible by 4
    1. The last two digits of the number 528 is 28, which is divisible by 4, therefore 528 is divisible by 4.
    2. The last two digits of the number 1468 is 68, which is divisible by 4, therefore 1468 is divisible by 4.
  4. Divisibility by 5 - ends with zero or five
    1. The last digit of 635 is 5, therefore it is divisible by 5.
    2. The last digit of 420 is 0, therefore it is divisible by 5.
  5. Divisibility by 6 - divisible by 2 and 3
    1. 222 is an even number which makes it divisible by 2. The sum of its digits is equal to six (2+2+2 = 6), which is divisible by 3. Since it is both divisible by 2 and 3, 222 is divisible by 6.
    2. 480 is an even number which makes it divisible by 2. The sum of its digits is equal to twelve (4+8+0 = 12), which is divisible by 3. Since it is both divisible by 2 and 3, 480 is divisible by 6.
  6. Divisibility by 8 - last three digits are divisible by 8
    1. The last three digits of 6328 is 328, which is divisible by 8, therefore 6328 is divisible by 8.
    2. The last three digits of 87416 is 416, which is divisible by 8, therefore 87416 is divisible by 8.
  7. Divisibility by 9 - sum of digits is divisible by 9
    1. The sum of 2736 is 18, which is divisible by 9, therefore 2736 is divisible by 9.
    2. The sum of 486 is 18, which is divisible by 9, therefore 486 is divisible by 9.
  8. Divisibility by 10 - ends with zero
    1. The last digit of 820 is zero, therefore it is divisible by 10.
    2. The last digit of 560 is zero, therefore it is divisible by 10.

Decimals

  1. Addition of Decimals - In adding decimals, write the given numbers so that the decimal point falls in line or arranged in one column. Then proceed as in addition of whole numbers and place the decimal point to the sum in the same column as the other decimal points.

    Add 32.8, 5.235, 122.2

                32.8
       +     5.235
           122.2
           --------
           160.235
  2. Subtraction of Decimals - Write the given numbers so that the decimal point falls in line or arranged in one column. Then proceed as in subtraction of whole numbers and place the decimal point to the difference in the same column as the other decimal points. If the minuend contains fewer figures after the decimal point than the subtrahend, annex zeroes.

    Subtract 2.34 from 12.5

                12.50
        -    2.34
            -----
            10.16
  3. Multiplication of Decimals - To multiply a decimal, multiply as in whole numbers; then, beginning from the right of the product, move the decimal point to the left depending on the number of decimal places of both factors.

    Multiply 53.165 by 3.02

                53.165
        ×     3.02
          ---------
          160.5583
  4. Division of Decimals - To divide a decimal, divide as in whole numbers and place the decimal point in the quotient above the point in the dividend.

    Example: Divide 81.5 by 2

    1. 81.5 ÷ 2 = 40.75
