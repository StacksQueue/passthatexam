LET Physical Science Coverage

1. Introduction - Nature of Science

This section provides an overview of the scientific method, principles of scientific inquiry, and the nature of science as a systematic and evidence-based approach to understanding the natural world.

2. Chemistry

Atomic and Molecular: Covers the structure of atoms and molecules, including atomic theory, periodic table trends, and molecular bonding.

Chemical Bonds: Explores the various types of chemical bonds, such as covalent and ionic bonds, and their significance in chemical reactions.

Conservation of Matter and Stoichiometry: Focuses on the law of conservation of mass and stoichiometric calculations in chemical reactions.

Gases, Liquids, and Solids: Examines the behavior of gases, liquids, and solids, including their properties and phase transitions.

Acids and Bases:Covers the concepts of acidity and alkalinity, pH, and acid-base reactions.

Solutions: Explores the properties of solutions, including solubility, concentration, and colligative properties.

Chemical Thermodynamics: Discusses principles of thermodynamics, including heat, energy transfer, entropy, and Gibbs free energy. properties.

Chemical Kinetics and Equilibrium: Focuses on reaction rates, factors affecting reaction rates, and chemical equilibrium.

3. Organic and Biochemistry

This area delves into the chemistry of carbon compounds (organic chemistry) and their relevance to biological systems (biochemistry). Topics may include organic functional groups, biochemical pathways, and biomolecules.

4. Nuclear Processes

Covers nuclear chemistry, including radioactivity, nuclear reactions, and the applications and implications of nuclear processes.

5. Physics

Physical Quantities and Vectors: Introduces fundamental physical quantities, measurement, and vector analysis.

Mechanics: Explores the principles of motion, forces, work, energy, and momentum.

Electricity, Magnetism, and Electronics: Focuses on electromagnetism, electrical circuits, magnetic fields, and electronic devices.

Thermodynamics: Discusses the laws of thermodynamics, heat engines, and thermodynamic processes.

Modern Physics: Examines the behavior of light, reflection, refraction, and optical phenomena.

Earth and Space, Astronomy, and Environment: Addresses concepts related to Earth and space science, including celestial bodies, planetary systems, and environmental issues.
